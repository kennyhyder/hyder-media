# How to monetize this playbook

You have a high-quality, real-world-tested asset. Now the question is what business model wraps around it. Five options below, scored against the constraints of a solo consultant who already has a marketing-services business (you).

## TL;DR — recommended

**Three-tier package** that lets one product serve three different buyer intents:

| Tier | Price | What they get | Buyer intent |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** (lead magnet) | $0 | 5-page condensed intro PDF, gated by email | Learns who you are; enters your funnel |
| **Playbook** (standalone product) | $79 | Full PDF + all templates + code snippets + Claude skill | DIY builder who wants the reference |
| **Done-for-you launch** (high-ticket service) | $2,500–5,000 | You apply the playbook to their project as a 2-week engagement | Doesn't want to learn, wants it done |

This is the model below — see `02-pricing-tiers.md` for the math. You can ship all three within 2-3 weeks of decision.

## The five monetization options, scored

### Option A — Pure lead magnet (free PDF, no paid tier)

**How it works:** Give the playbook away free in exchange for email. Use the email list to upsell consulting.

**Pros:**
- Maximum distribution. Free = wide reach = lots of email captures.
- Reinforces "Kenny is the expert" positioning for your existing consulting business.
- Low maintenance — one PDF, one landing page.

**Cons:**
- No direct revenue from the asset itself.
- "Free" undervalues the work; some buyers respect paid content more.
- Hard to measure ROI without a clear funnel.

**Best for:** Consultants who already have a strong consulting funnel and just need a top-of-funnel asset.

**Realistic year-1 revenue:** $20-50k from the consulting attribution. PDF itself contributes $0 but compounds into ~3-10 new consulting clients.

### Option B — Paid digital product ($79-149, one-time)

**How it works:** Sell the full playbook + templates as a downloadable bundle. Distribute via Gumroad / Lemon Squeezy / your own Stripe checkout. No consulting upsell required.

**Pros:**
- Direct revenue per buyer.
- Scales infinitely (digital good, no fulfillment cost).
- "Paid product" positions you as a published author, which lifts consulting rates anyway.

**Cons:**
- Lower volume than free.
- Requires landing page that justifies the price.
- Buyer support (refund requests, questions) is real labor.

**Best for:** Builders who want passive revenue with minimal client work.

**Realistic year-1 revenue:** 500–2,000 buyers × $79–149 = $40k–300k depending on distribution effort.

### Option C — Online cohort course ($199–2,000, time-boxed)

**How it works:** Turn the playbook into a 4-week cohort. Sell seats. Run live calls. Award a certificate.

**Pros:**
- Highest revenue per buyer if you can fill cohorts.
- Community + accountability = stronger outcomes for students = testimonials.
- Builds your brand as an authority in a way a static PDF can't.

**Cons:**
- Massive time commitment per cohort. Multiple hours/week for the entire 4 weeks.
- Requires marketing for each cohort launch. "Always-on" funnel is hard.
- High cancellation/refund rate at $500+ price points.

**Best for:** Educators who genuinely enjoy teaching live.

**Realistic year-1 revenue:** 2–4 cohorts × 20 seats × $799 = $32k–64k, minus 100+ hours of teaching time. Effective hourly rate often lower than consulting.

### Option D — Notion / GitBook template ($49-149)

**How it works:** Package the playbook as a Notion or GitBook duplicate template. Buyers click "Duplicate to my workspace" and own a copy.

**Pros:**
- Trendy distribution channel (Notion marketplace).
- Buyers can edit/customize directly.
- Lower price point makes impulse purchase viable.

**Cons:**
- Notion-specific; alienates buyers who use other tools.
- Hard to track usage / engagement after sale.
- Lower perceived value than a polished PDF.

**Best for:** Audiences who live in Notion (consultants, ops people, productivity nerds).

**Realistic year-1 revenue:** $5k–20k. Niche but recurring trickle.

### Option E — Membership / community ($19-49/mo recurring)

**How it works:** Gated Discord or Circle community. Monthly access to the playbook + AMAs + private Q&A.

**Pros:**
- Recurring revenue.
- Community network effect — members help each other.
- You can ship incremental playbook updates without re-launching.

**Cons:**
- Highest ongoing labor — you have to show up in the community.
- Churn is brutal (typical SaaS-style 5–10% monthly).
- Hard to start cold; need ~100 founding members to make it not feel empty.

**Best for:** Founders who genuinely enjoy community-building.

**Realistic year-1 revenue:** 50–200 members × $29/mo × 9 months avg retention = $13k–52k. Net of platform fees + your time, often breakeven year 1.

## Why the three-tier hybrid wins (for Kenny specifically)

You're a consultant. Your existing revenue engine is high-ticket client services. The playbook should reinforce that engine, not compete with it.

**Free tier** = top of funnel for your consulting business. Captures emails. Demonstrates expertise to people who'd consider hiring you.

**$79 paid tier** = "I read your stuff" trust signal at scale. People who don't have budget for consulting can still buy. Many of them become consulting clients 6-18 months later when their needs grow.

**$2,500–5,000 DFY service** = where the real money is. The playbook is the credibility asset that makes selling DFY easy. You don't have to convince anyone you know what you're doing — they read the playbook, they already know.

This stack lets one piece of content serve three buyer types:
- Curious (free)
- DIY committed ($79)
- Outsourcing ($2,500+)

You only need to maintain the content in one place (the playbook). Distribution + landing pages differ per tier.

## Revenue math (conservative)

Year 1 projections assuming you do the launch sequence in `04-launch-the-playbook-itself.md`:

**Distribution numbers:**
- 5,000 free PDF downloads (combination of organic traffic + paid promo)
- 2% paid conversion to $79 tier = 100 paid customers × $79 = **$7,900**
- 0.5% paid → DFY conversion = 25 DFY consultations × $3,500 avg = **$87,500**

**Total: ~$95,000 direct revenue**

Plus indirect:
- ~10 inbound consulting referrals from "read your playbook" credibility = $30k–100k+
- 3-5 speaking / podcast invitations that lift personal brand
- Inbound recruiting interest if you're hireable

This is conservative. Aggressive launch (Show HN, podcast tour, paid ads) doubles or triples the numbers.

## Recommended decision

**Build the three-tier stack.** Free is the lead magnet, $79 is the product, $3,500 is the service.

If you ONLY have time for one tier in the next 30 days, pick:

- **If revenue is the goal**: ship the $79 tier first. Free tier without paid funnel is unattributable.
- **If audience growth is the goal**: ship the free tier first. Get the email list growing.
- **If you're already overbooked with consulting**: skip the $79 tier and just use free PDF → DFY funnel.

Don't try to ship all three the same week. Sequence:

- **Week 1**: Free PDF + landing page + email capture. Get the funnel running.
- **Week 3**: $79 paid tier on Gumroad/Lemon Squeezy. Email the free list.
- **Week 5+**: DFY consulting offer added to the email sequence as upsell.
