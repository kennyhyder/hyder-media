The Site Selection Challenge

Datacenter developers evaluate hundreds of potential locations across dozens of variables—power availability, fiber connectivity, water access, natural hazard risk, labor markets, and tax incentives.

Today this process relies on fragmented data, expensive consultants, and months of manual research. Critical sites are missed. Bad sites consume resources.

Fragmented Data

Power, fiber, water, and risk data live in 16+ separate federal databases with incompatible formats

Slow Process

Traditional site selection takes 6–12 months of consultant-driven analysis per geography

Missed Opportunities

Greenfield sites near retired power plants and brownfield sites on decommissioned land go undetected

Three Pathways to Power

GridScout identifies sites across three distinct development strategies, each with unique advantages.

Substation

~30,000

Sites adjacent to existing grid substations with known power capacity, voltage, and interconnection infrastructure.

  • Known power capacity (MW)
  • Existing transmission access
  • Fastest speed to power

Greenfield

~9,000

Undeveloped corridor land near high-voltage transmission with IRA Energy Community and Opportunity Zone tax benefits.

  • IRA tax credit eligibility
  • Opportunity Zone overlays
  • Clean development canvas

Brownfield

~900

Retired power plants and industrial sites with existing grid connections, water infrastructure, and cleared land.

  • Pre-existing grid tie-in
  • Water cooling already on-site
  • Reduced permitting timeline
