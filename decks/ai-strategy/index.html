Why Now
The data we're already throwing away

Every Claude interaction today produces a free training signal.
We capture none of it.

The current loop

  • Owner asks Claude to edit their hero copy
  • Claude produces a draft
  • Owner accepts, edits, or rejects
  • → We log only "Claude ran"

What we should capture

  • Prompt + Claude's output (already there)
  • What the owner shipped after editing
  • Downstream metric movement (leads / conversion)
  • → Labeled preference data, free

Frontier model labs cannot get this data. ChatGPT cannot. Only AutomateDojo can.

The One Thing We Steal
Parallel Agent Reinforcement Learning

PARL is what Kimi got right.
It's what our orchestrator already does.

total_reward = α(t) · instantiation_reward + β(t) · finish_reward + γ · outcome_reward

Orchestrator

Master onboarding flow OR bulk-strategy dispatcher. Already decomposes 1 customer ask into 15+ parallel tool calls.

Sub-agents

Parallel tool calls: submit_citation, draft_review_reply, update_dojo_content, provision_phone…

Outcome reward

Customer-marked "launch checklist complete & approved." Real signal, not a proxy.

When we train the orchestrator with PARL, we stop being "a SaaS that uses Claude" and become a SaaS that beats Claude on its workload.

11 / 15
Discipline
Explicitly Out of Scope

The discipline list. Saying no is half the strategy.

  • Don't pre-train from scratch. $50M+, 12+ months, dedicated research team.
  • Don't reimplement MuonClip, Kimi Linear, or Attention Residuals. Pre-training tech. Adoption comes free with next-gen open bases.
  • Don't self-host K2.6 to "save money" before 10k+ dojos. Burns capital while losing the cost race.
  • Don't fine-tune without a golden eval set. 200 hand-graded golden requests come first. Always.
  • Don't train on customer data without TOS + opt-out. Legal and reputational radioactive. Fix at 4 customers, not 400.
  • Don't conflate "agentic" with "swarmy." Most customer asks want one good call. PARL training belongs on the onboarding orchestrator, not the chat box.

Execution
The 6-Month Build

Concrete shipping calendar.

Month 1

Foundation — migration 021, outcome capture UX, TOS, cost observatory, caching + batch + tier routing, banner price-fix.

Month 2

Golden set — 200 hand-graded examples, corpus export script, base-model eval (Qwen vs Llama).

Month 3

SFT v1 — review-reply LoRA on Qwen 3 14B. Shadow-route 10% of traffic. Compare blind.

Month 4

Multi-LoRA — vLLM serves N adapters on 1 GPU. Add ad-copy adapter. Promote on win.

Month 5

DPO — first preference-optimization cycle on (claude_output vs customer_edit) pairs. Add site-copy adapter.

Month 6

RLAIF + verticals — Opus-as-judge synthesizes preference data. First per-vertical adapter (martial arts).

Customer-feature shipping paused. AI infrastructure becomes the main thread. Full bandwidth.
