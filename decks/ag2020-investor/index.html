The geography

Arizona is the single best auto-glass market in the United States.

The numbers don't lie

Arizona files 10% of all US auto-glass claims despite having only 2% of the US population. More claims than California or Texas — combined.

  • Monsoon hail + chip-prone freeways
  • Year-round driving (no winter dormancy)
  • Population growth +1.5%/yr

The regulatory tailwind

Arizona Revised Statute § 20-263: insurers must offer $0-deductible glass coverage on any comprehensive policy. Insurers cannot raise premiums on glass-only claims.

  • Consumer friction to replace = essentially zero
  • Direct insurance billing relationships are the moat
  • $4.5B in recent AZ hail-related insured losses

Sources: NICB; ARS § 20-263; Insurance Journal (West, 2020)

Why this market hasn't been consolidated

Every independent shop fights blind — and loses to Safelite by default.

The four cuts that bleed independents

  • No attribution: Owners spend on Google, Meta, billboards, referrals — and have no idea which dollar earned which job.
  • No speed-to-lead: Web form fills sit in an inbox. Calls go to voicemail at lunch. Safelite picks the customer up.
  • No data integration: Ads platform, CRM, scheduling, billing, insurance EDI — five disconnected systems, three people retyping.
  • No transferable playbook: Owner retires; the business loses 30% in 18 months. No SOP, no acquirer.

"Most independent operators can't tell you their CAC, their LTV, or their ad ROAS to two decimal places. We can — and the spread is the opportunity."
