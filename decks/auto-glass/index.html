The ADAS Explosion

80%+
of new vehicles have at least one ADAS feature

Forward-facing cameras, radar sensors, and LiDAR units are now standard. Every windshield replacement on these vehicles requires calibration.

Calibration Economics

  • Cost: $300-800 per vehicle
  • Time: 2-3 hours labor
  • Equipment: $15K-50K per unit
  • Training: Specialized certification required
4 / 18

The Problem

Equipment Gap

Only 26% of workshops have ADAS calibration equipment. The other 74% lose the entire job.

Capital Barrier

$15K-50K per calibration unit is prohibitive for independent shops operating on thin margins.

Certification Chaos

Insurers now require certified procedures. No standardization across independent networks.

Insurance Pressure

"Insurance companies are starting to emphasize certifications and standards more. They want to ensure only qualified professionals handle repairs."

Insurers are liable if an uncalibrated ADAS system causes an accident. They have strong incentive to fund compliance infrastructure.

Key Risks & Mitigations

Safelite Response

They could aggressively compete or acquire.

Mitigation: FTC scrutiny limits M&A. Build insurer relationships they can't replicate.

Equipment Financing Risk

Default risk on equipment loans.

Mitigation: Equipment is collateral. Claims revenue is predictable. Partner with equipment finance specialists.

Insurance Partner Concentration

Dependence on few major insurers.

Mitigation: Multi-carrier strategy from start. Data products reduce dependence.

Tech Commoditization

Calibration equipment prices could drop.

Mitigation: Network effects compound. Data moat grows. Switching costs remain.
