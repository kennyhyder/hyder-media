Section 01

The Opportunity

SolarTrack is a purpose-built intelligence platform for the commercial solar aftermarket. It tracks every commercial and utility-scale solar installation in the United States — who built it, who owns it, what equipment is on site, and what's happened to it since it was installed.

For an equipment reseller like Blue Water Battery, this means being able to answer questions like: Which sites in Texas have 10+ year old inverters that are likely due for replacement? Who owns the 500 MW portfolio that just changed hands? Which installers in the Southeast are doing the most commercial work?

No other freely available database combines this depth of installation detail with equipment-level intelligence and site risk history. The closest commercial alternatives cost $20,000–$30,000 per year and cover less ground.

Why This Matters for Equipment Sales

The U.S. has ~150 GW of installed solar capacity. Panels degrade, inverters fail, hail damages arrays, and sites get repowered. SolarTrack identifies where the aging equipment is, who to contact about it, and what damage events may have accelerated replacement timelines. It turns the entire U.S. solar fleet into a prospecting database.

Equipment Aging Intelligence

The database tracks installation age alongside equipment model years. Sites with 10+ year old inverters, discontinued panel models, or equipment from manufacturers who have exited the market are flagged as high-probability replacement candidates.

Entity & Ownership Data — 240,000+ Entities

Four types of entities are tracked per installation: owner (who holds the asset), operator (the utility or entity managing the grid connection), developer (who built or financed the project), and installer (the EPC contractor).

Storm Damage = Replacement Leads

86% of all tracked installations have been in a county that experienced damaging hail or severe wind since 2015. Cross-referencing storm events with equipment age and manufacturer gives you a prioritized list of sites most likely to need panel or inverter replacement. No commercial solar database offers this.

Data Sources — 101 Aggregated Sources

Data is aggregated from every major free and public solar data source in the United States, plus selective purchased datasets.

Federal Government (7 sources)

EIA-860 + EIA-860M (federal generator census), USGS USPVDB (utility-scale photovoltaic), EPA RE-Powering (brownfield solar), BLM Solar ROW (federal land permits), FERC EQR (PPA contracts), Treasury Section 1603 (grant awards)

State Programs (11 sources)

CA DGStats, NY-Sun, IL Shines, MA PTS, MN PUC, PA AEPS, NC NCUC, NJ DEP, Virginia Cooper Center, NREL Community Solar, Hawaii Energy

ISO Interconnection Queues (7 ISOs)

CAISO, NYISO, ERCOT, ISO-NE, PJM, SPP, MISO — covering all 7 major grid operators

Municipal Building Permits (75 cities)

Scraped from Socrata, ArcGIS, CKAN, CARTO, OpenDataSoft, and BLDS portals across 75 U.S. cities and counties. Equipment details extracted from permit descriptions via NLP.

Purchased & Satellite Data

SEIA Major Projects List (8,400+ utility-scale projects), Microsoft Global Renewables Watch (satellite-detected solar polygons), LBNL Tracking the Sun (distributed solar)

Enrichment Layers (20+ cross-references)

Each installation is enriched with up to 20 additional data layers: NOAA storm history, CPSC equipment recalls, FEMA flood zones, parcel ownership records, CEC equipment specifications, reverse geocoding, cross-source deduplication, and more.

Section 06

The Road Ahead

SolarTrack has a clear development roadmap with diminishing cost per incremental improvement. The heavy infrastructure work is done — what remains is enrichment, integration, and productization.

Phase 1: Quick Wins (Minimal Cost)
Automated monthly data refreshes, additional permit portal scraping, equipment gap filling from permit descriptions, FEMA flood zone completion (~75%+ coverage expected). All infrastructure exists — just running existing scripts on new data.
Phase 2: Data Deepening ($1K–$5K)
SEIA Major Projects List refresh ($1K/yr), expanded satellite mount classification, additional parcel ownership lookups, FERC PPA enrichment for more utility-scale sites.
Phase 3: Product Evolution
API access for CRM integration, alerting system (new installations in your territory, equipment recalls, storm damage events), white-label portal for Blue Water Battery's customers, saved searches and watchlists.
Phase 4: Market Expansion
SaaS product for the broader solar aftermarket (insurers, O&M providers, equipment manufacturers). The database and infrastructure exist — productization is the remaining step.
