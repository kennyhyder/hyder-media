How Bail Works in Connecticut

Connecticut bail premiums are fixed by state law on a sliding scale — 10% of the first $5,000 of bond and 7% of everything above — and agents may not discount them.

After bail is set by a judge or bail schedule, there are three common ways to secure release:

  • Surety bond — a licensed bail bond agent posts the full bond for a non-refundable premium, typically 10% of first $5,000, 7% above of the bail amount.
  • Cash bond — the full bail amount paid directly to the court, refundable when the case concludes and all appearances are made.
  • Release on recognizance — release without payment at the court's discretion, sometimes with pretrial supervision.
  • Licensing: bail agents are regulated by the Connecticut Insurance Department.
