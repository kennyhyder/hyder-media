How Release Works in Oregon

Oregon abolished commercial bail in 1974 — defendants post a 10% security deposit directly with the court instead of using a bondsman.

Oregon does not permit commercial bail bondsmen. Release from custody after an arrest typically happens one of these ways:

  • Release on recognizance — a written promise to appear, no payment required.
  • Deposit or cash bond to the court — where money bond exists, it is paid directly to the court (often a 10% deposit), and is refundable at case disposition.
  • Supervised or conditional release — pretrial services supervision, check-ins, or other non-monetary conditions.

Because no bail bond companies operate here, be wary of anyone offering paid bail services in Oregon.
