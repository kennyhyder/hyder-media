How Release Works in Maine

Maine has no commercial bail bond industry — release is handled by bail commissioners, and any cash bail is paid directly to the court.

Maine does not permit commercial bail bondsmen. Release from custody after an arrest typically happens one of these ways:

  • Release on recognizance — a written promise to appear, no payment required.
  • Deposit or cash bond to the court — where money bond exists, it is paid directly to the court (often a 10% deposit), and is refundable at case disposition.
  • Supervised or conditional release — pretrial services supervision, check-ins, or other non-monetary conditions.

Because no bail bond companies operate here, be wary of anyone offering paid bail services in Maine.
