How Release Works in Wisconsin

Wisconsin abolished commercial bail bonding in 1979 — bail is posted in cash directly with the court, though a friend or family member may act as surety.

Wisconsin does not permit commercial bail bondsmen. Release from custody after an arrest typically happens one of these ways:

  • Release on recognizance — a written promise to appear, no payment required.
  • Deposit or cash bond to the court — where money bond exists, it is paid directly to the court (often a 10% deposit), and is refundable at case disposition.
  • Supervised or conditional release — pretrial services supervision, check-ins, or other non-monetary conditions.

Because no bail bond companies operate here, be wary of anyone offering paid bail services in Wisconsin.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a bail bondsman in Monroe County?

No — Wisconsin does not permit commercial bail bondsmen. Wisconsin abolished commercial bail bonding in 1979 — bail is posted in cash directly with the court, though a friend or family member may act as surety. Release is handled through the court: recognizance, deposit bond paid directly to the court, or supervised release.

How do I get someone out of jail in Monroe County?

Contact the court or pretrial services office handling the case. Where a deposit bond applies, it is paid directly to the court clerk and is largely refundable when the case concludes.
