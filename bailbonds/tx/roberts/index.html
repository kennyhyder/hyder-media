Bail Bond Licensing in Roberts County

Roberts County does not operate a bail bond board (required only above 110,000 population). Bondsmen here operate with the approval of the county sheriff.

How Bail Works in Texas

After an arrest in Texas, a magistrate must set bail promptly — the law requires a magistrate hearing within 48 hours of arrest (Tex. Code Crim. Proc. art. 15.17). Once bail is set, there are four ways to secure release:

  • Surety bond — a licensed bail bond company posts the full bond for a non-refundable premium, typically 10–15% of the bail amount.
  • Cash bond — the full bail amount is paid directly to the county, refundable when the case concludes and all appearances are made.
  • Personal recognizance (PR) bond — release without payment, at the court's discretion. Texas law (SB 6, 2021; SB 9, 2025) restricts PR bonds for many offenses.
  • Attorney bond — under Tex. Occ. Code §1704.163, a licensed Texas attorney may post bail for a client they represent in the criminal case, in any Texas county. One retainer can cover both the release and the defense.

Attorney Bonds — An Alternative to a Bondsman

Texas is one of the few states where a licensed attorney can post bail directly for a client they represent (Tex. Occ. Code §1704.163) — in Roberts County and every other Texas county. Instead of paying a bondsman a non-refundable 10–15% premium and separately hiring a defense lawyer, one call can handle both the jail release and the defense.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a bail bond cost in Roberts County?

Bail bond premiums in Texas typically run 10-15% of the total bail amount — the fee a licensed bail agent charges to post the full bond. It is not refundable. On a $10,000 bond, expect roughly $1,000 or more depending on the rate.

How long does it take to get released from jail in Roberts County?

Once a bond is posted, release typically takes 2–12 hours depending on the facility's booking volume. Bail must first be set by a judge or bail schedule, which usually happens within 24–48 hours of arrest.

What if I can't afford the bail bond premium?

Many bail agents offer payment plans, and some accept collateral (property, vehicles) in place of full cash payment. Courts may also lower bail at a hearing — a defense attorney can request it.

How do I find out if someone is in jail in Roberts County?

Contact the Roberts County sheriff's office or check its online inmate roster if one is published. You'll need the person's full legal name and, ideally, date of birth.

What is an attorney bond in Texas?

Under Texas Occupations Code §1704.163, a licensed Texas attorney can post bail for a client they represent in the criminal case — in any Texas county — without being a licensed bondsman. One retainer can cover both the jail release and the criminal defense.
