How Bail Works in North Carolina

North Carolina law caps bail bond premiums at 15% of the bond amount, enforced statewide by the Department of Insurance.

After bail is set by a judge or bail schedule, there are three common ways to secure release:

  • Surety bond — a licensed bail bond agent posts the full bond for a non-refundable premium, typically 10-15% of the bail amount.
  • Cash bond — the full bail amount paid directly to the court, refundable when the case concludes and all appearances are made.
  • Release on recognizance — release without payment at the court's discretion, sometimes with pretrial supervision.
  • Licensing: bail agents are regulated by the North Carolina Department of Insurance.
