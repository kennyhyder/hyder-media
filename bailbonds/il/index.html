How Release Works in Illinois

Illinois abolished cash bail entirely in September 2023 under the Pretrial Fairness Act — release is determined by a judge without money bond.

Illinois does not permit commercial bail bondsmen. Release from custody after an arrest typically happens one of these ways:

  • Release on recognizance — a written promise to appear, no payment required.
  • Deposit or cash bond to the court — where money bond exists, it is paid directly to the court (often a 10% deposit), and is refundable at case disposition.
  • Supervised or conditional release — pretrial services supervision, check-ins, or other non-monetary conditions.

Because no bail bond companies operate here, be wary of anyone offering paid bail services in Illinois.
