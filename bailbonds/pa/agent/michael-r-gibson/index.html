How Bail Works in Pennsylvania

Philadelphia courts release most defendants on a 10% deposit paid directly to the court, so commercial bondsmen operate mainly elsewhere in Pennsylvania.

After bail is set by a judge or bail schedule, there are three common ways to secure release:

  • Surety bond — a licensed bail bond agent posts the full bond for a non-refundable premium, typically 5-10% of the bail amount.
  • Cash bond — the full bail amount paid directly to the court, refundable when the case concludes and all appearances are made.
  • Release on recognizance — release without payment at the court's discretion, sometimes with pretrial supervision.
  • Licensing: bail agents are regulated by the Pennsylvania Insurance Department.
