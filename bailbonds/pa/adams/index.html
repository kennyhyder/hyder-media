How Bail Works in Pennsylvania

Philadelphia courts release most defendants on a 10% deposit paid directly to the court, so commercial bondsmen operate mainly elsewhere in Pennsylvania.

After bail is set by a judge or bail schedule, there are three common ways to secure release:

  • Surety bond — a licensed bail bond agent posts the full bond for a non-refundable premium, typically 5-10% of the bail amount.
  • Cash bond — the full bail amount paid directly to the court, refundable when the case concludes and all appearances are made.
  • Release on recognizance — release without payment at the court's discretion, sometimes with pretrial supervision.
  • Licensing: bail agents are regulated by the Pennsylvania Insurance Department.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a bail bond cost in Adams County?

Bail bond premiums in Pennsylvania typically run 5-10% of the total bail amount — the fee a licensed bail agent charges to post the full bond. It is not refundable. On a $10,000 bond, expect roughly $500 or more depending on the rate.

How long does it take to get released from jail in Adams County?

Once a bond is posted, release typically takes 2–12 hours depending on the facility's booking volume. Bail must first be set by a judge or bail schedule, which usually happens within 24–48 hours of arrest.

What if I can't afford the bail bond premium?

Many bail agents offer payment plans, and some accept collateral (property, vehicles) in place of full cash payment. Courts may also lower bail at a hearing — a defense attorney can request it.

How do I find out if someone is in jail in Adams County?

Contact the Adams County sheriff's office or check its online inmate roster if one is published. You'll need the person's full legal name and, ideally, date of birth.
