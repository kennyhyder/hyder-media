How Bail Works in Alabama

Alabama bail bondsmen are now licensed by the state's dedicated Professional Bail Bonding Board rather than the Department of Insurance.

After bail is set by a judge or bail schedule, there are three common ways to secure release:

  • Surety bond — a licensed bail bond agent posts the full bond for a non-refundable premium, typically 10-15% of the bail amount.
  • Cash bond — the full bail amount paid directly to the court, refundable when the case concludes and all appearances are made.
  • Release on recognizance — release without payment at the court's discretion, sometimes with pretrial supervision.
  • Licensing: bail agents are regulated by the Alabama Professional Bail Bonding Board.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a bail bond cost in Baldwin County?

Bail bond premiums in Alabama typically run 10-15% of the total bail amount — the fee a licensed bail agent charges to post the full bond. It is not refundable. On a $10,000 bond, expect roughly $1,000 or more depending on the rate.

How long does it take to get released from jail in Baldwin County?

Once a bond is posted, release typically takes 2–12 hours depending on the facility's booking volume. Bail must first be set by a judge or bail schedule, which usually happens within 24–48 hours of arrest.

What if I can't afford the bail bond premium?

Many bail agents offer payment plans, and some accept collateral (property, vehicles) in place of full cash payment. Courts may also lower bail at a hearing — a defense attorney can request it.

How do I find out if someone is in jail in Baldwin County?

Contact the Baldwin County sheriff's office or check its online inmate roster if one is published. You'll need the person's full legal name and, ideally, date of birth.
