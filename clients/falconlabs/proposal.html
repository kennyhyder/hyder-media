Section 04

Scope of Work

I'll own the marketing operations stack end to end: tracking foundation, three paid acquisition channels, the email program (its own channel, treated separately), and the dashboards that keep everything visible. Target audience throughout: established DTC brands at the 50K+ monthly-orders profile Falcon defined.

4.1 Conversion Tracking Foundation

The infrastructure layer that makes everything else trustworthy. Built first, deployed across all platforms.

  • GTM container architecture, with consolidated tags for GA4, Meta, LinkedIn, Google Ads, and server-side endpoints
  • GA4 event schema design and instrumentation
  • Meta Pixel + Conversions API deployment (server-side)
  • LinkedIn Insight Tag + server-side conversion events
  • Google Ads conversion tracking with enhanced conversions
  • Cross-channel deduplication and attribution modeling
  • Conversion validation across all platforms before any paid spend launches

4.2 Paid Acquisition Channels

Google Ads

Search-first, with YouTube layered where it earns its place. Built on the competitive intelligence work already completed.

  • Search campaign architecture for high-intent capture and category demand
  • Brand defense layer (Rokt, AfterSell conquest targeting where appropriate)
  • Non-brand category and intent capture
  • YouTube campaigns layered on top once tracking and audiences are seasoned
  • Ad copy aligned to Falcon's free install / rev-share differentiator
  • Landing page recommendations and copy iteration

Meta Ads

Audience seeding from existing CRM, lookalike scale, decision-maker layers.

  • Custom audience build from Falcon's existing merchant contact lists, since these are already the right customer profile
  • Lookalike modeling (1 to 3% quality and 5 to 10% scale tiers) seeded against Falcon's paying customer list
  • Layered B2B targeting against business decision-makers, e-commerce job titles, and Shopify Plus / mid-market DTC interest signals
  • Creative testing across video and static formats with founder-led, problem-framed angles
  • Lead Gen Forms as secondary low-friction capture

LinkedIn Ads

High-fit B2B reach against the mid-market DTC audience. LinkedIn is best suited to this profile.

  • Matched-audience upload of Falcon's CRM
  • Account-list targeting against curated mid-market DTC brand lists (50K+ monthly-orders profile)
  • Job-title targeting (Founder, Head of Growth, VP eCommerce, Director of CX)
  • Sponsored Content + Lead Gen Forms as primary formats; Document Ads for lead-magnet capture
  • Discovery sprint in month 2 to validate audience hypotheses before scaled spend

4.3 Email Program (built from zero)

Email is treated as its own complete channel, not a paid acquisition channel. Currently Falcon has no email program: transactional and customer communication is hand-sent through Google Workspace. This builds the full system from infrastructure up.

  • ESP selection and setup (Customer.io and Klaviyo as candidates, decided in week 1)
  • Domain authentication: SPF / DKIM / DMARC + dedicated sending subdomain
  • Sending IP warm-up plan
  • Migration of currently hand-sent transactional emails into automated flows
  • First three lifecycle flows: new-merchant onboarding, activation rescue, performance celebration
  • Monthly merchant newsletter program (Falcon supplies content and angle, I handle copy refinement, formatting, send, and reporting)
  • Behavior-triggered re-engagement and win-back
  • Apollo.io kept for outbound sales sequences; ESP layered for transactional, lifecycle, and marketing

4.4 Reporting & Operations Dashboards

Two custom-built dashboards that consolidate everything into one place: one for Falcon, one for day-to-day email operations.

Falcon Labs Performance Dashboard

  • Per-channel performance (Google, Meta, LinkedIn) with blended ROAS and CAC by channel
  • Email program metrics: deliverability, open / click / conversion rates by flow, list growth, cohort retention
  • Cross-channel attribution view with conversion-source breakdown
  • Spend pacing, budget forecast, and projection rollups

Email Operations Admin

  • Live flow status (which lifecycle sequences are firing, send volumes, errors)
  • Deliverability monitoring (bounce rate, spam complaints, IP / domain reputation)
  • A/B test queue and result tracking
  • List segmentation and hygiene tools

Both dashboards are custom-built software deployed on infrastructure under our control during the engagement and migrated to a Falcon Labs subdomain on signing. License terms covered in Section 09.

4.5 Ongoing Management (Monthly)

  • Per-channel optimization: bid management, audience refinement, creative iteration, search-query analysis, negative keyword management
  • Email: newsletter copy refinement and formatting (from Falcon-provided content), A/B test cadence, new flow build (one per quarter included), deliverability monitoring, list hygiene
  • Cross-channel attribution review and budget reallocation
  • Dashboard maintenance, data integrity checks, anomaly investigation

4.6 Reporting & Communication

  • Weekly performance snapshots (Monday morning KPI email)
  • Bi-weekly strategy calls (30 to 45 min)
  • Monthly performance report with cross-channel analysis and recommendations
  • Quarterly business review with forward strategy

Note on creative scope: I produce text copy and landing pages. Image and video creative for paid platforms sit outside my scope of production. Falcon's in-house designer can produce these, or I can refer creative partners I work with for that production.

My standard ad management minimum for managing a single ad account in isolation is $5,000/month. The $7,500 floor reflects that reality, and once spend scales, the percentage calculation takes over.

Section 06

Performance Targets

The primary paid-channel performance metric is blended CAC: ad spend across Google + Meta + LinkedIn divided by acquired merchants. Specific numeric targets are not preset in this proposal. Falcon does not yet have a paid-marketing CAC baseline, and any number written here today would be a guess.

Phase structure

Phase Months CAC target Description
Build & Test 1 to 3 No target Validate tracking, establish channel-by-channel CAC baselines, collect creative learnings
Prove 4 Set together at end of month 3 Demonstrate channel viability at agreed-upon CAC. Floor for any further spend ramp.
Scale 5 to 9 Set together at end of month 3 Steady-state target across all paid channels. Spend ramps gated to this CAC.
Optimize 10 to 12 Set together at end of month 3 Best-in-class efficiency at full velocity once channels have matured.
Why the targets defer

The Build & Test phase is where the CAC baseline gets measured. By end of month 3 there is real data: channel-by-channel CPCs, conversion rates, audience response, install-to-active-merchant rates. The Prove, Scale, and Optimize targets are then set together based on what that data shows is achievable.

This avoids writing speculative numbers into a contract. It also means the targets that govern the performance bonus (Section 07) and the spend scaling gates (Section 08) are anchored in reality, not in guesswork at proposal stage.

Email program metrics

Email is measured separately on its own KPIs and is excluded from the paid-channel performance bonus in Section 07:

  • Deliverability: inbox-placement rate and spam-complaint rate against industry benchmarks
  • Lifecycle conversion: onboarding-to-activation and activation-rescue rates (specific targets set at end of month 3 with real baselines)
  • List health: active subscriber growth and engagement-decay tracking
  • Newsletter performance: open and click rates by cohort, contribution to merchant retention
Section 07

Performance Incentive

To align interests beyond the base management fees, a quarterly performance bonus structure exists in principle. Specific bonus-trigger thresholds and rates are set together at the end of month 3, after the Build & Test phase has produced real CAC data.

Structure (specific numbers set together at end of month 3)

Floor (always applies): the engagement must be net positive for Falcon (annualized profit run-rate from acquired merchants exceeds total cost) before any bonus triggers. No bonus is paid when Falcon isn't ahead.

Two-tier upside: a base bonus tier and an exceptional-performance tier, both calibrated against the agreed Scale-phase CAC target. Lower CAC, higher bonus. Falcon retains the majority of the upside at every level.

Calculation basis: share of net profit, where net profit is the annualized profit run-rate from acquired merchants in the measurement period less total cost (ad spend + Hyder Media fees) in the same period.

Scope: Google + Meta + LinkedIn combined. Email is measured on its own metrics and excluded from the bonus calculation.

First eligible measurement period: months 4 to 6, assessed at end of month 6.

Illustrative example (using placeholder numbers)

The numbers below are placeholders to illustrate the structure, not commitments. The actual Scale CAC target ($X) and bonus-trigger thresholds are agreed at end of month 3 against measured baseline.

  • If CAC at agreed Scale target: $0 bonus. Falcon keeps 100% of the value created.
  • If CAC meaningfully below agreed Scale target: bonus triggers at Tier 1 rate (5% of net profit).
  • If CAC exceptionally below agreed Scale target: bonus triggers at Tier 2 rate (10% of net profit).

The base management fees are unchanged regardless of bonus eligibility. The bonus is upside only, never a floor. The "set together at end of month 3" commitment is documented as a binding addendum to the executed contract once baseline data is observed.

Section 08

Scaling Framework

Spend ramps are performance-gated to make sure unit economics hold before scaling. The specific gate thresholds are set together at end of month 3, against the same baseline data that anchors the Section 06 phase targets and Section 07 bonus thresholds.

The principle is straightforward: above a low-risk testing threshold, additional spend ramp requires that trailing-period CAC stays at or below the agreed Prove-phase target. Past the next threshold, it requires staying at or below the Scale-phase target. Falcon's capital doesn't get deployed at higher velocity than measured performance supports.

Section 10

Why This Structure

What this proposal optimizes for

Setup paid in two halves. $30K up front gets the foundation built before any spend launches. The $15K Launch invoice covers the going-live work: ad account builds, lifecycle flow deployments, dashboard v1 deliveries. Falcon isn't paying for 60 days of work in one lump.

Email priced flat and tiered on list size. No spend-percentage gamesmanship on a service that isn't spend-based. The tier steps up as the list scales because the work scales with it.

Paid media tiered marginally. The effective rate decreases as spend grows. At $400K/month combined spend, the blended rate is under 6%.

Performance targets defer to data. The Prove / Scale / Optimize CAC targets, bonus thresholds, and scaling gates get set together at end of month 3, against measured baseline. The proposal doesn't lock either party into speculative numbers before the data exists.

Performance bonus aligned to upside, not at-target performance. The bonus only triggers when measured CAC is meaningfully better than the agreed Scale target. At target, $0 bonus. Falcon keeps the majority of upside in every scenario.

Software license outlives the engagement. The dashboards and admin tools are Falcon's perpetually. If the engagement ends, you keep operating the Software. You don't get the ongoing support, updates, and hosting that come with an active engagement, but the software stays yours.
