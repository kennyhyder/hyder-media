Background & Purpose

This analysis was conducted on behalf of Falcon Labs (falconlabs.com) ahead of building a paid acquisition strategy for its post-purchase monetization platform. Falcon Labs turns e-commerce order-confirmation pages into a profit channel: AI-curated partner-brand offers shown post-checkout, on a pure rev-share model ($0.35–$0.45 per transaction to the merchant, zero cost to install). The Shopify app (“Falcon Post Purchase Offers,” launched September 2025) holds a 5.0★ rating, and the platform also integrates with Wix.

Because falconlabs.com is a young domain with no third-party traffic history (SimilarWeb has no data), this suite was built from primary research: a 872-keyword universe enriched with live Google Keyword Planner volumes and bids plus 12-month SimilarWeb paid-search exports for every competitor, direct teardowns of all six competitor landing pages, and ad-transparency research for each competitor — the same process used to launch and scale paid search programs for comparable two-sided platforms.

The competitive set spans three weight classes: Rokt (the category-defining enterprise player, 10B+ transactions/yr, owner of AfterSell, its 40,000-brand Shopify arm), the like-for-like challengers — Uptick (closest direct comp: thank-you-page offer network, ~$0.35/order pitch) and Disco (DTC cross-sell network) — and the adtech-rooted players: Fluent/AdFlow (public company, 1.2B+ commerce moments, Rebuy partnership) and MomentScience (fka AdsPostX, loyalty/perks angle).

Key Strategic Findings

  • The paid SERP is essentially undefended — confirmed by 12 months of SimilarWeb paid-search history. Uptick, Disco, and Fluent ran zero paid search keywords in the last 12 months. Rokt spent ~$160K but 96% of its paid clicks are brand-defense on its own name (only $2.3K/yr non-brand). AfterSell is the lone real spender (~$355K/yr, 32% non-brand). The non-brand category SERP is wide open.
  • The competitors themselves validate conquesting. AfterSell's #1 non-brand paid keyword is “rokt” ($7.3K/yr); MomentScience's entire paid program targets “rokt,” “aftersell by rokt,” and “fluent” ($21.7K/yr). Everyone conquests Rokt — and nobody defends against it.
  • The category keyword is cheap and uncontested. Core terms like “post purchase offers,” “thank you page ads,” and “order confirmation page monetization” show LOW competition in Keyword Planner — the giants sell via outbound/partnerships, leaving search open.
  • Conquesting is the volume play. “Rokt” (12,100/mo) and competitor-brand terms dominate the universe's search volume; Rokt's enterprise sales motion leaves SMB/mid-market searchers underserved — exactly Falcon's wedge.
  • "Alternatives" pages are the proven playbook. AfterSell runs dedicated /uptick-alternatives and /disco-alternatives pages — the comparison-intent demand is real and competitors are already harvesting it.
  • Shopify App Store is a parallel search channel. 77 Shopify-app keywords (~2,600/mo) feed both google.com and App Store search; Falcon's 5.0★ rating is an asset to amplify, not just defend.
  • Every competitor hides pricing. Falcon's “free install, pure rev-share, $0.35–$0.45/transaction” can be said out loud in ad copy — a differentiation no competitor ad currently makes.
