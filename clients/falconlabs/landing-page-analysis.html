Category Patterns & Falcon's LP Strategy

The "$ per transaction" number is the category's lingua franca

Uptick leads with "~$0.35 profit per transaction," Fluent claims "$0.35 per digital transaction," Falcon's own calculator shows "$0.35–$0.45." Buyers comparison-shop on this single number — Falcon's LP should anchor it in the hero, not bury it in a calculator.

Every competitor hides pricing — Falcon shouldn't

Zero of six pages show pricing. Falcon's model (free install, pure rev-share) is itself the pricing message. Saying "no cost, no contract, rev-share only" in the hero converts the category's biggest friction point into Falcon's headline differentiator.

Demo-gating is universal at the top; self-serve wins below

Rokt, Uptick, Fluent, MomentScience all gate behind "Talk to us / Request a demo." Only AfterSell offers "Start Free Trial." Falcon's Shopify one-click install is structurally more self-serve than everyone except AfterSell — the LP should let merchants install without a call.

Per-order earnings testimonials beat logos

Uptick's most persuasive elements are merchant quotes citing exact figures ("an additional $0.64 of bottom line profit per order"). Falcon has equivalent quotes in its Shopify reviews ("GREAT way to generate added revenue at no cost") — collect and quantify these early.

Calculator as conversion device

Both Falcon and Uptick embed revenue calculators. Uptick pairs theirs with a CTA ("Calculate My Revenue"). Falcon should make the calculator the ad landing experience for non-brand traffic: traffic in → personalized revenue estimate → install CTA.

Brand-safety language is the enterprise table stake

"Premium brand partnerships," "vetted advertisers," "without compromising brand integrity" appear across every page. Merchants' #1 fear is junk ads after checkout. Falcon's curation story needs named example brands as proof, not adjectives.
