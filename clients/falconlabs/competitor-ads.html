Live ad transparency links and company profiles for all six tracked competitors. Google Ads Transparency Center shows every search/display/YouTube ad a domain runs; Meta Ad Library shows active Facebook/Instagram creative. Use these to monitor messaging shifts before and after launch.

R
Rokt
rokt.com
Enterprise

The category creator and dominant player in post-transaction monetization. Powers Ticketmaster, PayPal, Uber, Domino's. Acquired AfterSell (2024) for SMB reach and mParticle (2025, ~$300M) for data infrastructure. Sells via enterprise sales motion — search is not their growth engine, which leaves the SERP open.

  • Claimed scale33,000+ clients · 10B+ transactions/yr
  • Brand search volume12,100/mo ("rokt")
  • ModelEnterprise contracts, AI "Brain"
  • Watch forMixed search intent: ETF + careers queries

A
AfterSell by Rokt
aftersell.com
Shopify mid-market

Rokt's Shopify arm — checkout/post-purchase upsell suite + "network ads" monetization. The most aggressive search marketer in the set: runs dedicated conquesting pages (/uptick-alternatives, /disco-alternatives). Their playbook is the blueprint for what works in this category's SERP.

  • Claimed scale40,000+ brands · $1B+ revenue generated
  • Brand search volume~1,090/mo (brand group)
  • ModelSaaS tiers + monetization rev-share
  • Watch forAlternatives/comparison LP playbook

U
Uptick
uptick.com
Direct comp

The closest like-for-like competitor: thank-you-page offer network, one-line install, ~$0.35 profit/transaction pitch, platform-agnostic (Shopify, BigCommerce, Magento, WooCommerce, custom). Founded by Rob Jewell (Power Digital). Their messaging and calculator UX are the closest analog to Falcon's own positioning.

  • Claimed scale100+ brands (Good American, Corkcicle)
  • Brand search volume5,400/mo ("uptick" — partly ambiguous)
  • ModelRev-share offer network
  • Watch for"$0.61–0.64/order" testimonial claims

D
Disco
disconetwork.com
DTC network

DTC-brand cross-sell network (DiscoFeed) repositioning as a "Commerce Media Network for Post-Purchase & Beyond." A16Z-backed. Network is built from peer DTC brands recommending each other — a different supply model than Falcon's curated premium-brand offers, which is a positioning lever against them.

  • Claimed scale~1,000 DTC brands (reported)
  • Brand search volume~240/mo (brand group)
  • ModelBrand-to-brand cross-sell network
  • Watch forAfterSell actively conquests them

F
Fluent / AdFlow
fluentco.com
Public adtech

Public adtech company (NASDAQ: FLNT) pivoted hard into commerce media. AdFlow is the post-purchase product; the Rebuy partnership ("Rebuy Ads powered by Fluent") gives them embedded Shopify distribution. Two-sided pitch mirrors Falcon's: "I want to advertise" / "I want to monetize."

  • Claimed scale1.2B+ commerce moments · 200M profiles
  • Brand search volume~200/mo (product terms)
  • ModelCommerce media marketplace
  • Watch for"$0.35 per digital transaction" claim — identical number to Uptick/Falcon

M
MomentScience
momentscience.com
Perks / loyalty

Formerly AdsPostX. AI post-transaction advertising with a loyalty/perks angle ("MomentPerks") — placements across homepage, cart, checkout, post-purchase email. Targets retail loyalty programs more than pure e-commerce checkout. Claims 130M+ moments served, 3–5x CPM over standard IAB units.

  • Claimed scale130M+ moments · Costco, Hulu, SiriusXM
  • Brand search volume~160/mo (brand group)
  • ModelPerks network + APIs
  • Watch forRebrand splits their search equity (adspostx → momentscience)

Adjacent players worth monitoring (compete for the same thank-you-page real estate with own-product upsells rather than network offers): ReConvert (reconvert.io, 590/mo brand volume), Zipify OneClickUpsell, Honeycomb Upsell, Carro, and Paylode (perks marketing, 260/mo). If a merchant installs one of these first, the thank-you page is occupied — they are conquesting targets for "alternative" campaigns even though their business model differs.
