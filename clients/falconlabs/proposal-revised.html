Paid-Channel Engagement — Revised

Three Paths Forward

Paid-channel focus, email deferred, conversion tracking implemented by Falcon engineering. Three structural options sized to a $25K/month initial ad budget, with expanded performance upside.

Section 01

Where We Landed

Three structural changes from prior versions, all reflecting Falcon's feedback. The result is a meaningfully smaller engagement sized to the stated $25K/month ad budget.

What changed since the prior options doc

Email program deferred. Per Falcon's request, the email program is removed from this scope and revisited later. The original argument for building email first was attribution — without a way to capture and track leads, ad spend can't be tied to outcomes. That gap is now filled by the next change.

Conversion tracking implemented by Falcon engineering. Hyder Media defines the conversion event spec, server-side API requirements, and data layer needs. Falcon's engineering team implements them on the site, app, and Apollo.io integration. Hyder Media validates the implementation end-to-end before any paid spend launches. This is the substitute for the email-based attribution layer.

Sized to a $25K/month ad budget. All three paths assume Falcon's stated initial monthly budget. Setup and management costs are scaled accordingly, and the per-platform marginal fee tiers from prior versions still apply as ad spend scales beyond initial budget.

Full technical approach, channel-by-channel build detail, and dashboard architecture remain consistent with the original proposal at hyder.me/falcon-proposal except for the email program (removed) and tracking ownership (transferred to Falcon engineering).

Section 02

Revised Scope Summary

What stays, what's gone, what shifts ownership.

What Hyder Media still owns

  • Conversion event spec and data-layer requirements document (input to Falcon engineering)
  • Server-side API configuration on the platform side: Meta Conversions API, LinkedIn server events, Google enhanced conversions
  • End-to-end validation of tracking implementation before any paid spend launches
  • Google Ads campaign architecture, audience build, launch, and ongoing management
  • Meta Ads campaign architecture, audience build (CRM lookalikes), launch, and ongoing management
  • LinkedIn Ads campaign architecture, account-list targeting build, launch, and ongoing management
  • Cross-platform performance dashboard for Falcon leadership reporting
  • Site and stack optimization (SEO/AEO) included as part of foundation work
  • Weekly KPI snapshots, bi-weekly strategy calls, monthly performance reports, quarterly business reviews

What Falcon engineering now owns

  • Implementation of the conversion event tracking on the Falcon website / app (GTM tags, event firing, data-layer pushes per spec)
  • Apollo.io integration to capture attribution data on inbound leads
  • Any product-side data emission that the spec calls for (signup events, install events, paid-customer events, etc.)

What's deferred

  • Email service provider selection, setup, and warm-up
  • Domain authentication and dedicated sending infrastructure
  • Lifecycle email flow design and build
  • Monthly email program management retainer
  • Email admin dashboard
Existing Google Ads infrastructure

Falcon mentioned existing Google Ads infrastructure that can be reviewed in more detail. Week 1 of any path includes a discovery audit of what's already in place. If existing campaigns, audiences, or conversion configurations are usable as-is or with adjustments, Hyder Media will credit the time savings against the Google Ads setup fee on a documented basis (typical savings $1K–$3K depending on what's reusable).

Section 07

Expanded Performance Upside

The performance bonus is the compensating mechanism for Falcon's structural concessions. Triggers are intentionally accessible. Upside percentages are doubled from the original proposal. Falcon retains the majority of upside at every tier.

Three deliberately accessible tiers

Floor (always applies): the engagement must be net positive for Falcon — annualized profit run-rate from acquired merchants exceeds total cost — before any bonus tier triggers. No bonus when Falcon isn't ahead.

Tier 1 — 10% of net profit: trailing 3-month blended CAC is at or below the Scale-phase target. Matching the target triggers bonus — not "beat target by X%".

Tier 2 — 15% of net profit: trailing 3-month blended CAC is 20% below the Scale-phase target.

Tier 3 — 20% of net profit: trailing 3-month blended CAC is 40% below the Scale-phase target.

Net profit defined: (merchants acquired in period × $63,000 annualized LTV) − (total ad spend + Hyder Media fees in period).

How the Scale target gets set: deliberately conservative

At end of month 3, the Build & Test phase has produced trailing 60-day blended CAC across the platform(s) live at that point. The Scale-phase target is set at that actual blended CAC × 1.25 — a 25% buffer above what was already achieved.

Practical impact: if actual trailing CAC during Build & Test is $4,000, the Scale target lands at $5,000. Tier 1 bonus triggers when ongoing performance matches the original $4,000 actual. Tier 2 triggers at $4,000 (20% below the $5,000 target). Tier 3 triggers at $3,000 (40% below). The target is calibrated for upside, not gatekeeping.

Mechanics

  • Measurement period: rolling trailing 3 months, assessed at end of each calendar quarter
  • First eligible period: months 4–6, assessed at end of month 6
  • Scope: all active paid channels combined (Google + Meta + LinkedIn as they come online)
  • Bonus invoiced: within 15 days of quarter close, Net 15 payment terms
  • Falcon's share at every tier: Tier 1 = Falcon keeps 90%, Tier 2 = Falcon keeps 85%, Tier 3 = Falcon keeps 80%
Section 08

Terms

Inheriting from the original proposal Section 09 except where modified here.

What changed for the revised paid-channel-only scope

  • Email program removed from scope. Can be added back via a separate scope-of-work amendment at any time during the engagement.
  • Conversion tracking implementation ownership transferred to Falcon engineering, with Hyder Media owning spec, server-side platform configuration, and validation.
  • Minimum term length: 18 months (Path A), 12 months (Path B), or 24 months (Path C), versus 12 in original
  • Foundation invoice: $14K (Paths A and C) or $15K (Path B), down from $30K in original
  • Monthly paid-media-management minimum: $7,500 once all three platforms are active, or $5,000 during single-platform phases (down from $12,500 total in original which included $5K email retainer)
  • Performance bonus structure expanded per Section 07

What didn't change

Account ownership, perpetual irrevocable software license treatment, confidentiality, reporting transparency, invoicing terms (Net 15), early termination provisions all carry forward from the original proposal Section 09 unchanged.
