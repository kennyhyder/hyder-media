Cross-competitor patterns

  • Premium brands hide pricing (ClickFunnels, Kajabi) — they sell the dream first, sticker shock is gated. PageWheel can do the opposite and lead with $27 as a competitive wedge.
  • All-in-one platforms emphasize tool replacement (Kartra, GoHighLevel) — "replaces 8-12 tools." This is not PageWheel's lane — PageWheel sells speed-to-launch, not feature breadth.
  • Systeme.io leads with free — direct counter to PageWheel's $27. PageWheel's wedge against Systeme: AI-first templates + done-for-you setup vs Systeme's bare-bones DIY breadth.
  • Kajabi is aspirational — sells the prestige of being a knowledge entrepreneur. Different positioning entirely; PageWheel can win the "I just want to ship something" buyer that Kajabi over-sells to.
  • Video heroes dominate at the premium tier (CF, Kartra, Kajabi, GHL). At PageWheel's price point, a clear hero image + 30-second demo is sufficient and converts faster.

PageWheel Landing Page Roadmap

Nine landing pages across three phases, anchored in the cleaned non-brand keyword universe (35,724 pure-intent kws after filtering competitor brands, WordPress/CMS noise, and AI-tool noise). Phase 1 has full briefs ready for build. Phases 2 and 3 are scoped at the keyword cluster level.

Strategic direction: lead with AI, not "landing page builder"

The largest commercial-intent volume sits in the broad "landing page builder" cluster (~50K/mo), but ClickFunnels, LeadPages, and every category incumbent already own that positioning. PageWheel is the only player whose product can credibly claim AI-first generation as the lead, not a feature buried on a tour page.

The AI-assisted cluster (~15K/mo of high-intent searches) is uncontested and grows fast. The roadmap leads with it, then layers in the broader category and persona-specific pages once PageWheel owns AI-first messaging.

What to defer (intentionally not in Phase 1–3)

  • All competitor brand-comparison LPs (/clickfunnels-alternative, /kajabi-vs-pagewheel, etc.) — strong intent but premature without first establishing PageWheel's positioning and trust signals. Revisit as Phase 4 once Phase 1–3 LPs are converting.
  • "Free landing page builder" (18,100/mo) — high volume but the searcher intent is "I don't want to pay." Burns budget on non-converters unless PageWheel has a freemium tier or genuine "free starter templates" hook. Skip until a freemium offer is decided.
  • WordPress / Gutenberg / Divi adjacent — different buyer mindset (WP ecosystem loyalty), won't convert to PageWheel. Not a fit even with massive volume.
  • Generic "AI" search (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.) — adjacent but not commercial-intent for page-building. The AI-assisted cluster in Phase 1 captures the real buyers without burning on broad-AI search.

What the 9 LPs together unlock

~155K monthly addressable searches across the full Phase 1–3 roadmap, at average ~$9 blended CPC. At conservative 2% LP conversion across paid + organic traffic, this is the foundation of a substantial, defensible pipeline that puts PageWheel in front of buyers in three distinct mindsets: "I want an AI tool" (Phase 1), "I am a [persona] needing this" (Phase 2), "I want to learn how to do X" (Phase 3).

Brand-competitor conquesting (Phase 4) layers on top of this foundation later, not before it.
