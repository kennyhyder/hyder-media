EU Expansion Keyword Analysis

Volume analysis for keywords US-based vendors might use to research expanding into European markets. Tests 660 EU-modifier variations against the 5 ad groups currently producing the bulk of vendor signups, plus 35 standalone EU-expansion phrases.

Generated June 23, 2026 · Source: Google Keyword Planner API, US geo-targeting, English

Verdict

The search demand to support a paid-search-led EU-expansion campaign does not exist.

US-based vendors are not actively searching for terms like "sell digital products in Europe", "selling to European customers", or country-specific equivalents. 97% of tested variants return zero measurable search volume. Only one keyword exceeds 500 monthly searches ("eu digital services act"), and it's regulatory research intent, not commercial intent.

Even at maximum match-type breadth across all 13 keywords with any measurable volume, the combined addressable US search universe for EU-expansion intent is roughly 800 monthly searches. After accounting for non-commercial intent (regulation lookups), the genuinely actionable pool is approximately 120 searches/month.

This is too small to anchor a paid search initiative. Search can play a supporting role, but it cannot be the primary channel. Recommended alternatives in the section below.

Where search CAN play a role

Honest answer: it's a "capture what's there" play, not a primary channel.

A small, low-budget campaign targeting the 6–7 commercial-intent terms above can capture this audience efficiently. Estimated reasonable spend ceiling: $500–$1,000 per month against this micro-pool. Anything more than that is wasted impressions.

Specific recommendation: One new ad group ("EU Expansion - Catch-All") under the existing Nonbrand campaign, exact + phrase match against the 6 commercial terms, low max CPC, send to a dedicated /sell-in-europe landing page that explicitly addresses US-vendor EU concerns (VAT compliance via OSS, payment processing, language localization, distance-selling rules).

What needs to drive the EU-expansion initiative instead

If search demand existed, we'd lead with it. It doesn't, so we have to reach this audience proactively.

1Email to existing US vendor base

Highest leverage

DG24 already has a list of active US vendors who are NOT currently selling in EU. This is the most concentrated, highest-intent audience for this initiative.

Tactic: 4–6 email lifecycle sequence: "your product is ready for EU", VAT-OSS made simple, payment + language details, social proof from US vendors who've expanded, soft activation CTA.

2LinkedIn Ads to US digital-seller titles

Audience known, reachable

"Course creator", "Digital product creator", "Ecommerce founder", "Course business owner" — all reachable on LinkedIn even if they don't search for "sell in Europe".

Tactic: Lead Gen Forms with EU-expansion guide PDF; Sponsored Content with US vendor case studies; retarget existing US vendor list via Matched Audiences.

3Sponsored content / partnerships

Authority + intent

Sponsored placements in newsletters and on sites the US digital-seller audience reads: Indie Hackers, Creator Economy reports, Foundr, Smart Passive Income. Co-publish with payment processors or VAT-compliance vendors who already have the audience's attention.

4SEO content for adjacent intent

Long-term compound

Capture US sellers who are searching adjacent terms: "vat for digital products", "selling to europe sales tax", "ecommerce international expansion". Publish authoritative guides on dg24.com with strong commercial follow-through.

5Geo flip: target EU buyers directly

Different audience entirely

Separate initiative worth considering: instead of asking US vendors to expand to EU, run paid search in EU markets ("vender productos digitales", "online digitale produkte verkaufen", "vendre des produits numeriques") to attract EU vendors to DG24 directly. Volume in native EU languages will dwarf the US-to-EU expansion search universe.

6Direct sales outreach to high-GMV US vendors

Concentrated value

For DG24's top US vendors by GMV, the activation value of expanding them into EU is high enough to justify named-account outreach. White-glove EU activation support as a sales motion, not a marketing one.

Methodology

For each of the 5 top-performing ad groups (by 90-day spend and conversions), 6–8 of the highest-performing seed keywords were extracted. 19 suffix patterns ({base} europe, {base} in eu, {base} uk, etc.) and 2 prefix patterns (european {base}, eu {base}) were applied to each seed, plus 35 standalone EU-expansion phrases (expand to europe, vat for digital products eu, sell to european customers, etc.). Total: 660 unique keyword variations.

Each variation was sent through Google's Keyword Planner API at batch size 15 (the validated stable batch size for this account) with exactOnly: true matching against US geo (geoTargetConstants/2840), English language. Returned data: average monthly searches (12-month trailing), competition tier, top-of-page bid range.

"Zero volume" in this analysis means Google has no recorded searches for the phrase in the last 12 months in the US. KP buckets low-volume terms into a "10/mo" floor, so a result of 10 means somewhere between 1 and ~50 actual searches. The 800/mo total addressable estimate is therefore the high end of the realistic range.
